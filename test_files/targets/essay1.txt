Machine learning is a branch of artificial intelligence that enables computer systems to learn and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed. It focuses on developing computer algorithms that can access data and use it to learn for themselves. The process begins with observations or data, such as examples, direct experience, or instruction, so that computers can look for patterns in data and make better decisions in the future.

The primary goal of machine learning is to allow computers to learn automatically without human intervention or assistance, and to adjust actions accordingly. Machine learning algorithms are trained on large datasets, and through this training process they identify statistical patterns, correlations, and relationships within the data.

There are three main types of machine learning: supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning. In supervised learning, the algorithm is trained on labelled data where the correct output is already known. In unsupervised learning, the algorithm discovers hidden patterns or data groupings without the need for labels. Reinforcement learning uses a reward-based system where the agent learns to make decisions by receiving positive or negative feedback from the environment.

Deep learning is a specialized subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers to model complex patterns in data. These deep neural networks are capable of learning representations of data with multiple levels of abstraction. Convolutional neural networks are commonly used for image recognition tasks, while recurrent neural networks are often applied to sequential data such as natural language processing.

The applications of machine learning are vast and continue to grow across many industries. In healthcare, machine learning models are used to detect diseases from medical imaging and predict patient outcomes. In finance, algorithms identify fraudulent transactions and optimize investment strategies. In transportation, machine learning powers autonomous vehicles and traffic control systems. The technology is fundamentally transforming how businesses operate and how scientific research is conducted around the world.
