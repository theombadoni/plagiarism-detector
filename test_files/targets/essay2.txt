Artificial intelligence encompasses a field known as machine learning, which gives computer systems the ability to learn and improve through experience rather than explicit programming. Algorithms are developed to access datasets and autonomously derive knowledge from them. By starting with observations, direct experience, or structured examples, these systems identify patterns and enhance their future decision-making capabilities.

Machine learning algorithms are exposed to large volumes of training data, through which they discover statistical patterns, correlations, and meaningful relationships. The overarching aim is for computers to adapt and improve their performance independently, without requiring ongoing human guidance or intervention.

Supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning represent the three fundamental categories of machine learning. Supervised approaches rely on labelled training examples with known correct outputs. Unsupervised methods allow algorithms to discover hidden groupings or structures within unlabelled data. Reinforcement learning trains agents through a feedback mechanism of rewards and penalties that guide decision-making over time.

Neural networks with numerous hidden layers form the foundation of deep learning, which is itself a specialized subset of machine learning. Such networks learn hierarchical representations and complex abstractions from raw data. Image recognition tasks frequently employ convolutional neural networks, while recurrent architectures are well suited to sequential problems including natural language processing.

Machine learning is transforming a wide range of industries through its diverse applications. Medical imaging analysis and patient outcome prediction are among the healthcare applications being developed. Financial institutions use algorithmic models to detect fraudulent activity and refine investment strategies. Self-driving vehicles and intelligent traffic systems rely on machine learning in the transportation sector. Scientific research and business operations are being fundamentally reshaped by these technologies.
